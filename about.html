About Adam

Our Story

Adam was born and raised in our district and attended local public schools. After serving our country overseas, he came home determined to fight for working people. For years he has volunteered with veterans groups, mentored at‑risk youth and organized food drives. Adam knows our communities because he’s lived our struggles and worked tirelessly to make life better for our neighbors.

Experience and Service

As a veteran and project manager, Adam understands the importance of leadership, accountability and teamwork. He has served on the boards of local nonprofits and partnered with labor organizations to promote good‑paying jobs and safe working conditions. His professional experience managing complex projects in the public and private sectors has prepared him to tackle our district’s toughest challenges—from rebuilding infrastructure to expanding access to healthcare.

Values

  • Fairness and justice for every person
  • Investment in education and our children’s future
  • Accessible, affordable healthcare for all families
  • Safeguarding our environment for future generations

How Adam Differs From the Incumbent

  • No special interest money: Adam refuses corporate and PAC money, while our current representative has accepted hundreds of thousands of dollars from political action committees.
  • Accountability: Adam pledges to share every vote and expenditure with you; the incumbent has operated behind closed doors and has deep ties to the corporate lobby.
  • Working‑family focus: Adam comes from a working‑class family and knows the cost of living crisis firsthand. The incumbent spent years leading the Seattle Chamber of Commerce and opposed a tax on large corporations designed to address homelessness.
  • Real change: Adam supports fair taxation of the ultra‑rich, universal healthcare and meaningful climate action. Our opponent promises progress but continues to side with wealthy donors.
