Meet Adam

WA-10 deserves representation that answers to you.

I’m a father, a veteran, and a public infrastructure leader. I’ve spent my career in environments where results matter and excuses do not.

I’m running because families here are working harder every year and getting less security in return. That is not inevitable. It is a choice. WA-10 deserves a representative who listens first and answers to the community, not wealthy donors or outside interests.

Public office is a job. It should come with standards.

Service built on responsibility

I spent more than 20 years in aviation and emergency operations, leading teams, solving hard problems, and keeping people safe. Today I oversee public infrastructure projects communities rely on every day.

I approach public office the same way I approached every mission: do the work, own the outcome, and never forget who you serve.

Why I’m running

When something matters, you do not just talk about it. You use your leverage to move it. That is the standard working people live under every day, and it should be the standard in Congress too.

My campaign is people-funded because representation should not come with a donor list attached. If I take the oath, my priorities will be set by WA-10, not by corporate PAC checks.

What you can expect from me

Not slogans. Clear standards you can hold me to.

  • I will put votes and accountability ahead of press cycles.
  • I will be direct with the public about what I support and why.
  • I will hold regular public town halls and show my work.
  • I will keep this campaign people-funded, without corporate PAC money or AIPAC money.

Rooted in WA-10

My family lives in Spanaway. We deal with the same pressures everyone else feels: rising costs, packed schools, and a future that gets harder to plan for.

I budget for childcare, housing, and gas just like everyone else here. When costs go up, it hits my family too. That is why this campaign is not a résumé project. It is responsibility.

Help set a higher standard in WA-10

If you want a representative who answers to voters and treats this job like a job, join us. Volunteer, spread the word, or chip in what you can.
