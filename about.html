Meet Adam

WA-10 is home to military families at JBLM, workers moving freight through the Port of Tacoma, state employees in Olympia, and people commuting the I-5 corridor from Spanaway to South Tacoma. Adam’s commitment is rooted in service to these communities—not donors.

  • No corporate PAC money
  • Transparent and specific: bill numbers, votes, and receipts
  • Working-family standard: action, not press releases

Credibility through lived experience

Adam understands the pressure families feel when costs rise faster than wages. That experience drives a discipline-focused approach: set priorities, publish progress, and accept accountability when outcomes lag.

He believes public service means telling the truth clearly, even when it is uncomfortable, and then doing the work required to move legislation.

Service standard

Accessible

Regular constituent updates and direct channels for community input.

Specific

Named bills, public votes, and clear timelines.

Accountable

If something gets blocked, explain why and state the next step.
