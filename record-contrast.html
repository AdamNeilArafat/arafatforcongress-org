Representation, not management.

In WA-10—JBLM families, Port of Tacoma workers, Olympia employees, I-5 commuters, and communities like Spanaway and South Tacoma—people need advocates who take risks for results, not managers of the status quo.

  • No corporate PAC money
  • Transparent and specific: bill numbers, votes, and receipts
  • Working-family standard: action, not press releases

Where incentives point

When campaigns rely heavily on political committee money, voters should ask what priorities get delayed. Loss aversion is real: every cycle of inaction costs families more in rent, healthcare, and commute stress.

Our standard is simple: if a bill helps working families, co-sponsor it, fight for it, and report progress in public.
