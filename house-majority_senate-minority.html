7) Contempt and compliance

  • Plain talk: if an agency refuses to comply, use fines, court action, or other penalties to compel action.
  • House option: adopt a rule that imposes daily monetary penalties on officials who defy subpoenas after due process.
Example: a privileged resolution that starts daily fines until documents are produced, while a funding rider blocks travel for the office that is in defiance.

8) Calendar Wednesday rescue

  • Plain talk: a rarely used path that lets a committee call up a reported bill on a Wednesday when leadership blocks it.
  • Use it for: narrow transparency or compliance bills that deserve daylight.
Example: bring up a short bill that requires public posting of spending plans within seven days.

Automatic triggers that do not need a new vote

  • Self executing gates: funds stay locked unless the agency posts required data and notifies the House clerk.
  • Certification switches: no travel or training funds for an office until the Inspector General certifies compliance.
  • Clock starts now: a rule submission starts a fast review window without leadership permission.
  • Auto referral: missed deadlines trigger a public legal review and a report to the House.

Measurements and bright line tests

  • Deficit guardrail: no increase to the on budget deficit in the five year and ten year windows under official scoring, unless fully offset within three fiscal years in the same bill.
  • Tax fairness test: the average effective federal tax rate for the top ten percent must not fall below the rate for the middle quintile in official tables.
  • Benefit share test: at least sixty percent of net benefits must go to the bottom sixty percent of households.
  • No recreation spending by officials: no public funds for club dues, greens fees, or similar recreation for senior officials. Security and protection are funded and reported separately.
  • Transparency timers: documents due in fourteen legislative days. Missed deadlines escalate to fines and a small account freeze in the next funding bill.

Pass or fail mapping inside the Evergreen Pact

Pass path

  • Rider passes, post a public tracker within seven days, hold an oversight check at day thirty.
  • CRA passes one chamber, publish the vote record, turn to enforcement riders, support court review with public records.

Fail path

  • Rider blocked in the Senate, split it into narrower accounts and attach to the next must pass item.
  • Subpoena ignored, begin daily fines after due process and freeze a travel or training account in the next bill.
  • Emergency vote fails, time limit the spending tied to that emergency and force a second vote on a short fuse.
