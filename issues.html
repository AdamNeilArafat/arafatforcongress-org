Universal Healthcare

Healthcare should be a basic human right. The United States is the only one of the 38 nations in the OECD (Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development) that does not offer universal healthcare, despite being the wealthiest. I support a single-payer system that guarantees coverage for everyone, reduces administrative costs, and improves health outcomes. It is time we joined the rest of the developed world and made healthcare about people, not profits.

Cost of Living

We need to lower the cost of essentials—housing, food, healthcare, childcare, and energy. This includes eliminating junk fees, capping prescription drug prices, and investing in supply chains that benefit workers and consumers, not monopolies. We can also support targeted subsidies to farmers and producers to stabilize food costs and reduce waste.

Housing

Everyone deserves a safe place to live. One way the federal government could help is by offering first-time homebuyers access to special mortgage rates and low down payments. I also support increased investment in public and mixed-income housing, and stronger renter protections.

Fair Taxation

Our tax code is rigged to reward billionaires and punish working families. I support closing loopholes, ending carried interest exemptions, and restoring higher rates on corporations and ultra-wealthy individuals. I would also support indexing key benefits and tax credits to regional cost differences. San Francisco is not Kansas—thresholds should reflect reality.

Reproductive Freedom

Reproductive healthcare is essential healthcare. I support full access to abortion, contraception, and medically necessary procedures—without fear, stigma, or delay. This is about freedom and bodily autonomy, plain and simple.

No Special Interests

Our politics are broken because too many in Congress answer to donors instead of the people. I won’t take a cent from corporate PACs, lobbyists, or billionaire Super PACs. We need to restore trust and independence in public office.

Corporate Accountability

I believe in rewarding companies that invest in good jobs and penalizing those that harm workers, dodge taxes, or pollute our communities. Corporate power should be checked, not coddled.

Climate and Energy

We need bold, urgent action to reduce emissions and accelerate the transition to clean energy. I support ending fossil fuel subsidies, investing in green jobs, and modernizing infrastructure to protect against climate impacts.

Public Campaign Financing

Seattle’s Democracy Voucher program shows how public financing can work. I support a national version that gives small donors the power to compete with billionaires. Voters—not donors—should decide elections.
