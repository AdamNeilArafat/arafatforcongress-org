Lowering the cost of living

Housing, groceries, childcare, energy. Everything keeps rising except paychecks. Families need relief that lasts.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support a federal ban on price gouging in essential goods with real penalties. This reins in excessive markups that squeeze working families.
  • I would support more federal funding and tax credits for affordable housing tied to strong labor standards. More homes lowers rent pressure.
  • I want to see caps on out-of-pocket costs for essential medicines and limits on hospital monopoly pricing. Lower medical costs free up family budgets.

Universal healthcare

No family should lose a home because someone got sick.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support universal coverage with automatic enrollment and a simple benefits floor. Fewer gaps means fewer bankruptcies.
  • I want to see direct drug price negotiation and an end to patent games. Lower drug prices hit household budgets immediately.
  • I would support boosting primary care and mental health funding in underserved areas. Preventive care saves money and lives.

Fair taxation

Close loopholes so working families don’t subsidize the largest corporations.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support a strong corporate minimum tax on book profits. If profits are reported to Wall Street, taxes shouldn’t be zero.
  • I would support anti-offshoring rules that end profit shifting and shell games. Keeping revenue here funds local priorities.
  • I want to see targeted tax relief for families and small businesses. More take-home pay helps Main Street, not just shareholders.

No special interests or lobbyists

Politics should serve people, not donors.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would not take corporate PAC money. Voters come first.
  • I would support a ban on members trading individual stocks while in office. No more conflicts of interest.
  • I want to see real-time disclosure of lobbyist meetings and public calendars. Sunlight builds trust.

Corporate accountability

Good jobs and safe workplaces should be the norm.

Corporate Social Responsibility Tax

What it is: a federal surtax applied when corporations push costs onto the public or act in bad faith.

  • Triggers: extreme CEO-to-median-worker pay gaps, chronic low wages below local living-wage benchmarks, repeated safety violations, mass layoffs during record profits, environmental damage.
  • How it works: the surtax rises with the harm; companies reduce it by raising wages, narrowing pay gaps, investing in safety, and remedying damage.
  • Why it helps: it rewards firms that do right by workers and communities and makes it costly to treat people and places as expendable.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support stronger penalties for wage theft and repeat OSHA violators.
  • I would support incentives for firms that create living-wage jobs in the U.S.
  • I want to see transparent reporting on pay ratios, safety, and community impacts so investors and the public can hold companies accountable.

Support farmers and fair trade

Keep agriculture strong and trade fair for workers and small producers.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support investment in local processing and cold storage. Shorter supply chains cut costs.
  • I want to see trade deals that enforce labor and environmental standards. No more race to the bottom.
  • I would support antitrust action where a few buyers squeeze farmers.

Climate action now

Protect what we love and create thousands of clean energy jobs.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support scaling wind, solar, and transmission while cutting bottlenecks in permitting.
  • I want to see home weatherization and heat-pump incentives that lower bills fast.
  • I would support holding major polluters responsible for cleanup and health costs.
