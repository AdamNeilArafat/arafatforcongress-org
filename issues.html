Lowering the cost of living

Housing, groceries, childcare, energy. Everything keeps rising except paychecks. Families need relief that lasts.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support a federal ban on price gouging in essential goods with real penalties.
  • I would support more federal funding and tax credits for affordable housing tied to strong labor standards.
  • I want to see caps on out-of-pocket costs for essential medicines and limits on hospital monopoly pricing.

Universal healthcare

No family should lose a home because someone got sick.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support universal coverage with automatic enrollment and a simple benefits floor.
  • I want to see direct drug price negotiation and an end to patent games.
  • I would support boosting primary care and mental health funding in underserved areas.

Fair taxation

Close loopholes so working families don’t subsidize the largest corporations.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support a strong corporate minimum tax on book profits.
  • I would support anti-offshoring rules that end profit shifting and shell games.
  • I want to see targeted tax relief for families and small businesses.

No special interests or lobbyists

Politics should serve people, not donors.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would not take corporate PAC money.
  • I would support a ban on members trading individual stocks while in office.
  • I want to see real-time disclosure of lobbyist meetings and public calendars.

Corporate accountability

Good jobs and safe workplaces should be the norm.

Corporate Social Responsibility Tax

What it is: a federal surtax applied when corporations push costs onto the public or act in bad faith.

  • Triggers: extreme CEO-to-median-worker pay gaps, chronic low wages, repeated safety violations, mass layoffs during record profits, environmental damage.
  • How it works: the surtax rises with harm; firms reduce it by raising wages, narrowing pay gaps, investing in safety, and remedying damage.
  • Why it helps: it rewards companies that do right by workers and communities and makes it costly to treat people and places as expendable.

What I support at the federal level

  • I would support stronger penalties for wage theft and repeat OSHA violators.
  • I would support incentives for firms that create living-wage jobs in the U.S.
  • I want to see transparent reporting on pay ratios, safety, and community impacts.
