Executive Summary

Here’s the core point: when something truly matters, you prove it. You co-sponsor the bill. You organize support. You use your leverage to move it. That’s not leadership. That’s the baseline.

Right now, even that baseline isn’t being met. Some of the most basic working-family legislation hasn’t earned co-sponsorship. That’s not oversight. That’s a choice.

This isn’t about leading every bill. It’s about meeting the minimum standard of effort. If you won’t even put your name on the work, you’re not trying to move it.

WA-10 deserves a representative who treats this like a job, not commentary. Do the baseline. Then fight to win.

Co-sponsoring a bill, even symbolically, isn’t enough. The standard is simple: if you support what working families want, you use your leverage to move it.

When bills are ready to advance, they should have our full support. Press statements alone don’t protect healthcare. Action does.

People over donors. If you want urgency in Congress, help us build the pressure that money can’t buy.

The Difference

Bills don’t “stall.” Members stall them.

When leadership wants something to pass, it becomes must-pass. It gets attached to moving packages. It becomes a red line. That’s what urgency looks like.

Stalling is a political decision, not a procedural mystery. The power exists to force movement when members choose to use it.

Working families can’t wait on another press cycle. If the priority is real, the leverage has to be real too.
