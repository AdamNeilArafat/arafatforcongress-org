Lowering the Cost of Living

Make housing and childcare attainable, groceries fair, and utilities affordable. Protect Social Security. Keep working families whole.

  • Housing supply & permitting: Faster digital approvals, flexible zoning near jobs/transit, support modular builds. Housing Supply & Permitting Bill First-Time Homebuyer 3% Mortgage Bill
  • Groceries & fair markets: Stop junk fees and MFNs. Standardize fee/contract disclosures. Strengthen regional competition. Fair Food & Grocery Competition Bill
  • Childcare: Expand and refund the CDCTC. Grow quality seat supply. Affordable Child & Dependent Care
  • Utilities: Bigger rebates for weatherization, heat pumps, and community solar; streamline LIHEAP. Energy Savings & Lower Bills
  • Social Security: End taxes on most benefits; fair top-end contributions; protect solvency. Working Families Solvency Bill
  • Good jobs access: Licensing reciprocity, apprenticeships, earn-while-you-learn. Good Jobs & Skills Bill

Target

By 2028, cut combined housing + childcare + utility costs by 15% for working families.

Funding

Close corporate loopholes and wasteful subsidies. No new taxes on working families.

Equity

Prioritize relief for rural families, seniors, and communities of color most hit by rising costs.

Strengths

Tangible kitchen-table relief focused on monthly bills.

Weaknesses

Permitting/zoning rely on state and local partners; timelines can slip.

Opportunities

Bipartisan room on childcare credits, apprenticeships, and LIHEAP streamlining.

Threats

Grocer/lobby pushback; interest-rate swings affecting housing affordability.

Universal Healthcare

Coverage that follows you for life. Lower drug prices. Real mental-health parity. Primary care close to home.

  • Automatic coverage: A national benefits floor so no one falls through the cracks.
  • Lower drug prices: Expand negotiation; stop patent gaming.
  • Parity & access: Enforce mental-health parity; grow clinics and workforce in care deserts.
  • Health in all policies: Align food, water, air, and workplace standards to health outcomes.

See the Universal Healthcare Bill (2027) · See sources

Target

Universal enrollment by 2030; reduce out-of-pocket costs by 20% for working families.

Funding

Shift subsidies from private-insurance middlemen; leverage bulk drug purchasing.

Equity

Rural towns, veterans, seniors, and people with disabilities get priority access.

Strengths

Automatic enrollment ends coverage gaps; drug savings are popular and proven.

Weaknesses

Complex transition; requires phased rollout and strong federal-state coordination.

Opportunities

Use price-negotiation wins and parity enforcement to build momentum.

Threats

Industry litigation and disinfo; budget scoring headwinds.

End Corruption & Special Interests

Stop pay-to-play. Make elections people-powered with real-time transparency and real enforcement.

  • Ban corporate PAC & lobbyist bundling; tighten coordination rules.
  • People-first financing: vouchers and small donations at the center.
  • Near real-time disclosure: public, machine-readable, with real penalties.
  • Close dark-money routes: beneficial-owner and pass-through disclosure.
  • Modernize the FEC: timely, enforceable decisions and referrals.

Clean Campaigns & Elections Bill · Congressional Vote Justification Bill · See sources

Target

By 2026, pass a clean-elections act that bans corporate PACs.

Funding

End lobbying tax breaks; rely on grassroots-funded campaigns.

Equity

Every voter’s voice counts equally, no matter income or ZIP code.

Strengths

Clear contrast; aligns with public demand for accountability.

Weaknesses

Process reforms can feel abstract; tie to pocketbook wins.

Opportunities

Leverage city/state models (e.g., vouchers) as proof points.

Threats

Tax Fairness, CSR, and Good Jobs

Close loopholes at the top. Hold chronic violators accountable. Open more paths to good-paying work.

  • Fair tax rules: 15% book-profit minimum; end offshoring games; expand CTC/EITC; simple fairness credit. Tax Fairness & Relief Bill
  • Corporate responsibility: Surtax for wage theft, extreme pay gaps, repeat OSHA/EPA harm; credits for good actors; standardized disclosures. CSR Bill
  • Good jobs & skills: Licensing reciprocity; apprenticeships; community-college/employer alignment; training incentives. Good Jobs & Skills Bill

Target

Grow middle-income take-home pay by 5–7% by 2029 via credits, training, and fair-tax enforcement.

Funding

Collect on book-profit minimums and repeat-violator surtaxes; reinvest in workforce pipelines.

Equity

Direct skills dollars to high-need ZIPs; report gains publicly each quarter.

Strengths

Pairs enforcement with pro-worker incentives; measurable revenue sources.

Weaknesses

Complex implementation; risk of compliance drag on small firms without carve-outs.

Opportunities

Broad support for child credits and skills pipelines; pragmatic wins.

Threats

Corporate lobbying; offshoring workarounds if enforcement is weak.
