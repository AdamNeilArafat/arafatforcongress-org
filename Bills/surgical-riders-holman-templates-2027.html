Surgical Riders and Narrow Conditions

Precise riders that fence funds, require conditions, or bar a specific action without rewriting an entire program.

Targeted Enforceable Fair Process

At a Glance

  • What it is: Narrow constraints in an appropriations bill to control a defined action.
  • Why it helps: Stops a single abuse while leaving the rest of a program intact.
  • Safeguards: Limited scope, timelines, and independent checks.
  • Who is covered: Programs and offices funded in the underlying bill.

When to Use

  • Specific action: You need to pause finalization of a rule or contract.
  • Transparency gap: A publication or record duty is being ignored.
  • Short fuse: A decision is imminent and broader legislation will not pass in time.
Explanation: Choose this tool when the listed conditions match your goal and other routes are too slow or too broad.

How it Works

  1. Draft: Identify the exact action to fence and write a clear 'none of the funds' clause.
  2. Carve outs: Preserve life safety and court-ordered compliance.
  3. Verification: Assign the inspector general or the Government Accountability Office a short review window.
  4. Sunset: Add an automatic end date or a renewal vote.
Explanation: These steps focus on speed, clarity, and a documented record the public can follow.

Simple Examples

  • Rule pause: None of the funds may be used to finalize Rule [Citation] until a 90 day public comment period is completed.
  • Contract bar: None of the funds may be used to award Contract [Number] until a competitive procurement is certified by the inspector general.
  • Publication duty: None of the funds may be used to implement [Action] until a machine readable dataset is posted with fields A, B, and C.
Explanation: Examples show narrow, real world uses you can adapt quickly.

Risks and Mitigations

  • Collateral impact: Write carve outs for safety and legal compliance.
  • Litigation: Keep the record and rationale clear and factual.
  • Workarounds: Define 'funds' to include money, supplies, staff time, information technology, and facilities.
Explanation: Mitigations keep the tool fair and effective while reducing conflict and delay.

Copy Ready Rider Text

None of the funds appropriated or otherwise made available by this Act may be used to finalize, implement, or enforce [Specific Action], until [Verification Step] is completed and reported to the Committees on Appropriations and posted on a public website in machine readable form. For purposes of this section, the term "funds" includes money, supplies, staff time, information technology services, and the use of federal buildings and other facilities.
