Energy Savings and Lower Bills Bill (2027)

Lower household energy bills by boosting efficiency, cutting waste, and ensuring programs are accountable to the public.

Efficiency Upgrades Lower Utility Bills Cleaner Energy GAO Audits

Section 1. Short Title

“This Bill may be cited as the Energy Savings and Lower Bills Act of 2027.”

Section 2. Program Investments

  • Support weatherization and efficiency retrofits for households under 150% of area median income.
    Explanation: Puts savings in reach for working families and renters.
  • Fund community energy co-ops and small-scale renewables.
    Explanation: Keeps energy dollars local and stable.

Section 3. Committee Report Directives (Oversight Follow-Through)

Each appropriation and authorization report shall include binding directives to ensure savings programs deliver measurable results and stay transparent.

Sample Directive Text:
The Department shall publish, within 60 days of obligation, a public dashboard reporting:
  (1) households served by income band,
  (2) average utility bill reduction achieved,
  (3) contractor performance scores,
  (4) geographic distribution of funds.
GAO shall audit compliance annually and certify public accuracy of the dashboard data.

See full Committee Directives & GAO Guards Toolkit

Section 4. Results Tracking

  • Require GAO to verify average annual household savings within 18 months.
  • Inspector General to report on fraud prevention and contractor oversight.
