No Net Increase Guardrail

A fiscal test that requires bills to be neutral to the national debt over five- and ten-year windows, with short-term offsets if scoring shows a shortfall.

Targeted Enforceable Fair Process

At a Glance

  • What it is: A scoring rule and offset requirement tied to bill passage.
  • Why it helps: Keeps promises honest and protects the budget.
  • Safeguards: Clear windows, automatic offsets, and a public scoring note.
  • Who is covered: Committees and sponsors of bills scored for budget impact.

When to Use

  • Fiscal discipline: You want neutral debt impact over standard scoring windows.
  • Transparency: Voters should see how a bill pays for itself.
  • Fallback: Use when the baseline may shift after enactment.
Explanation: Choose this tool when the listed conditions match your goal and other routes are too slow or too broad.

How it Works

  1. Score: Use five- and ten-year windows for budget impact.
  2. Test: If scoring shows a shortfall, select offsets due within three fiscal years.
  3. Publish: Attach a scoring note with the offsets before floor consideration.
  4. Review: Require a one-year check and automatic corrections if needed.
Explanation: These steps focus on speed, clarity, and a documented record the public can follow.

Simple Examples

  • Offset menu: Adjust excise rebates, close a narrow loophole, or reduce a duplicative subsidy.
  • Time bound: Offsets begin in the next fiscal year to avoid mid-year disruption.
  • Transparency: Post the scoring note and offsets in a machine readable format.
Explanation: Examples show narrow, real world uses you can adapt quickly.

Risks and Mitigations

  • Gaming: Use independent scorers and publish assumptions.
  • Over-correction: Limit offsets to the shortfall amount.
  • Delay: Set a deadline for publishing the scoring note before the vote.
Explanation: Mitigations keep the tool fair and effective while reducing conflict and delay.

Copy Ready Guardrail Text

It shall not be in order to consider a bill or joint resolution unless a public scoring note certifies that the measure does not increase net debt within the five-year and ten-year budget windows. If a shortfall is certified after enactment, the Secretary shall implement offsets from the savings menu identified by the Committees on the Budget in an amount equal to the shortfall within three fiscal years, and shall publish a notice of implementation within 30 days.
