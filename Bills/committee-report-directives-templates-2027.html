Committee Report Directives — Templates

Boilerplate structures to embed oversight conditions, timelines, and publication standards in committee reports for Appropriations and Authorization bills.

Targeted Enforceable Fair Process

At a Glance

  • What it is: Ready templates for deadlines, audits, and publication requirements.
  • Why it helps: Speeds drafting and ensures consistent, enforceable expectations.
  • Safeguards: Short timelines, clear outputs, and public posting links.
  • Who is covered: Agencies, programs, and recipients funded by the bill.

When to Use

  • Recurring updates: You need quarterly reports while funds are executed.
  • Transparency: The public should see contracts, waivers, or rule changes quickly.
  • Independent checks: Outside audits are needed before major steps.
Explanation: Choose this tool when the listed conditions match your goal and other routes are too slow or too broad.

How it Works

  1. Select: Choose a template and fill in the blanks for agency, deadline, and outputs.
  2. Attach: Place in the appropriate section of the report and cross-reference the bill text.
  3. Notify: Send to the agency head, general counsel, and liaison the day the report is filed.
  4. Track: Maintain a public dashboard that shows due dates and delivery status.
Explanation: These steps focus on speed, clarity, and a documented record the public can follow.

Simple Examples

  • Quarterly performance: The Committee directs [Agency] to submit a quarterly dashboard with cost, schedule, scope, and risks for [Program].
  • Contract inventory: The Committee directs [Agency] to publish a machine readable list of all contracts over one million dollars within 30 days.
  • Rulemaking timeline: The Committee directs [Agency] to post an annual schedule with planned notices and final rules with a 90 day update cadence.
Explanation: Examples show narrow, real world uses you can adapt quickly.

Risks and Mitigations

  • Ambiguity: Avoid vague verbs. Use 'shall' and list precise outputs.
  • Noncompliance: Tie to hearing dates and request staff-level briefings until delivery.
  • Redaction games: Require machine readable formats and field-level definitions.
Explanation: Mitigations keep the tool fair and effective while reducing conflict and delay.

Copy Ready Report Templates

Quarterly Dashboard. The Committee directs [Agency] to submit to the Committees on Appropriations and to publish on a public website a quarterly dashboard for [Program], including baseline and current cost, schedule, scope, and top five risks. The first dashboard is due 60 days after enactment. Publication of Contracts. The Committee directs [Agency] to publish, within 30 days after enactment and monthly thereafter, a machine readable list of all contracts over $1,000,000, including vendor, purpose, authority, amount, period of performance, and status. Pre-Award Review. The Committee directs the inspector general of [Agency] to review the procurement record and proposed award for [Contract] and to report any material concerns within 15 days prior to award.
