Bill Blueprint

Social Security – Working Families Retirement Security and Solvency Act

Legislative Blueprint with Explanations

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.

This Act may be cited as the “Social Security – Working Families Retirement Security and Solvency Act.”
Explanation: The title signals two priorities: helping working families and guaranteeing the program stays strong for decades to come.

SEC. 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSE.

  • Trustees report a 75-year actuarial shortfall of 3.82 percent of taxable payroll, threatening a reduction in earned benefits beginning in 2034.
    Explanation: Without action, retirement checks would shrink by about 20% in less than a decade.
  • Working families earning under $400,000 annually should not face new payroll tax burdens.
    Explanation: Keeps everyday workers safe from higher taxes while strengthening the system at the top.
  • Earned benefits are currently taxed as income, reducing net retirement checks for millions of middle-class seniors.
    Explanation: Fixes the “double taxation” problem and lets retirees keep more of what they’ve paid in.
  • Reforms should end benefit taxation for the middle class, keep benefits secure for current and future generations, and close the solvency gap with modest contributions from very high earners, investment income, and large estates.
    Explanation: Shifts the burden upward instead of cutting checks for seniors or raising taxes on working families.

TITLE I — RETIREMENT CHECK RELIEF

SEC. 101. ELIMINATION OF TAXATION ON BENEFITS FOR MIDDLE-INCOME RETIREES.

  • Social Security checks will no longer be taxed for households under $250,000 (joint) or $125,000 (single).
    Explanation: Nearly 40% of retirees now pay federal tax on their benefits. This change restores the promise of a full, untaxed retirement check for almost all seniors.
  • Households above those thresholds may still pay tax on a portion of benefits.
    Explanation: Keeps fairness by applying benefit taxation only at the very top of the income scale.

TITLE II — FAIR CONTRIBUTIONS ABOVE $400,000

SEC. 201. PAYROLL TAX DOUGHNUT ABOVE $400,000.

  • Applies the 6.2% Social Security contribution again on wages above $400,000.
    Explanation: Right now, a CEO stops paying in after about $168,600. Restarting above $400K makes sure the top keeps paying their share to keep the program strong.
  • Credits those earnings toward benefits, but with progressive adjustments.
    Explanation: Ensures fairness: high earners paying more in still get credit, but the formula stays tilted to protect average workers.

SEC. 202. CONTRIBUTION ON NET INVESTMENT INCOME ABOVE $400,000.

  • Adds a 12.4% contribution on capital gains, dividends, and other investment income above $400,000 (joint) / $200,000 (single).
    Explanation: Extends contributions beyond wages so wealthy households with large portfolios also help lock in retirement security.
  • Coordinates with the existing 3.8% investment surtax.
    Explanation: Prevents double taxation by adjusting IRS rules so the taxes don’t stack unfairly.

TITLE III — ESTATE AND GIFT TAX FAIRNESS

SEC. 301. RESTORATION OF 2009 ESTATE AND GIFT TAX PARAMETERS; DEDICATION OF RECEIPTS.

  • Restores estate and gift tax exemptions and rates to 2009 levels.
    Explanation: The current $13M+ exemption shields nearly all fortunes. Rolling back to $3.5M ensures ultra‑wealthy estates contribute to keeping the promise of Social Security.
  • Sends all estate and gift tax receipts directly into the trust funds.
    Explanation: Ties intergenerational wealth transfers directly to strengthening intergenerational retirement security.

TITLE IV — COVERAGE AND ADMINISTRATION

SEC. 401. COVERAGE OF NEWLY HIRED STATE AND LOCAL EMPLOYEES.

  • Requires new state and local employees (starting 2027) to be covered by Social Security.
    Explanation: Broadens the contributor base so the system is stronger for everyone.

SEC. 402. CONFORMING AMENDMENTS.

  • Directs Treasury and SSA to update regulations and reconcile with prior laws.
    Explanation: Ensures smooth integration and avoids conflicts with older statutes.

TITLE V — EFFECTIVE DATES

SEC. 501. EFFECTIVE DATES.

  • Benefit tax relief (Title I) applies beginning 2027.
    Explanation: Retirees see a bigger check almost immediately.
  • Payroll, investment, and estate provisions (Titles II–III) start in 2027.
    Explanation: Gives time for high earners and estates to plan ahead.
  • State/local coverage (Title IV) begins Jan. 1, 2027.
    Explanation: Aligns with the tax year for easy administration.

Messaging Alternatives

Cooler framing options instead of “protect Social Security”:

  • Keep the promise of Social Security
  • Guarantee retirement security
  • Lock in earned benefits
  • Make Social Security rock‑solid
  • Keep your retirement check safe
