No Net Increase Guardrail - Template

Requires legislation to meet a no net increase test in the five year and ten year windows. Offsets must be current law changes due within three fiscal years if a shortfall is identified.

Guidance

  • Use as a freestanding bill or as a rule change in a budget resolution or special rule.
  • Use CBO current law baseline and exclude trust fund reclassifications.
  • Pair with a tax fairness test page to avoid distributional regressions.

Template Text

Sec. 1. No measure shall be considered unless a Congressional Budget Office estimate shows no net increase to the unified budget deficit over the period of fiscal years 1 through 5 and 1 through 10.
Sec. 2. If an estimate shows a net increase, the measure shall identify current law changes that fully offset the increase, with such changes taking effect not later than the end of the third fiscal year beginning after enactment.
Sec. 3. The estimate shall exclude one time timing shifts and changes in classification of receipts or outlays that do not reflect changes in underlying law.
Sec. 4. The House may waive this requirement only by a recorded vote of two thirds of the Members present and voting.
