Good Governance Cost Neutral or Better Fair and Transparent Enforceable Clear Results

At a Glance

  • Cost: Neutral overall with targeted administrative costs offset by savings and reduced waste.
  • Impact: Improves fairness, transparency, and service delivery for the public.
  • Who is covered: Agencies, regulated parties, and the public who rely on these services.
  • Safeguards: Life safety carve outs, sunsets and reviews, and court enforcement lanes.

Why it matters

This bill sets clear standards. It reduces gamesmanship. It gives the public a fair, timely, and enforceable process.

Will this slow down urgent work?

No. Urgent safety work and court ordered compliance continue with narrow and renewable certifications.

Is there new bureaucracy?

No. The approach is simple. It uses short certifications and public notice backed by independent checks.

Does this change taxes?

No. The focus is on standards, fairness, and better execution. Any costs are covered by savings and recovery of waste.

National Emergencies Act - Termination (Joint Resolution)
JOINT RESOLUTION
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,
That the national emergency declared on [DATE] by Proclamation [NUMBER] is hereby terminated.
War Powers Resolution - Removal (Concurrent Resolution, House Vehicle)
CONCURRENT RESOLUTION
Resolved by the House of Representatives (the Senate concurring),
That, pursuant to section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution (50 United StatesC. 1544(c)), the President shall remove United States Armed Forces from hostilities in [THEATER] not later than [XX] days after the adoption of this resolution, unless Congress enacts a specific authorization.
War Powers - Removal (Joint Resolution to Present)
JOINT RESOLUTION
Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. Short title; findings; purpose. [Optional]
SEC. 2. Removal from hostilities. The President shall remove United States Armed Forces from hostilities in [THEATER] not later than [XX] days after the enactment of this joint resolution, unless Congress enacts a specific authorization.
