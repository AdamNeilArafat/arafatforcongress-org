TITLE I - CONTINUITY OF GOVERNMENT & OPERATIONS
SEC. 101. DEFINITIONS; “IMPEACHMENT PERIOD”.
“Impeachment period” means the time from House adoption of articles of impeachment until final Senate verdict, dismissal, or expiration of 90 days without Senate action, whichever occurs first. “Major action” means: (1) new emergency declaration; (2) new executive order with substantial budgetary or legal effect; (3) rule designated “major” under 5 United StatesC. 804; (4) reprogramming/transfer above agency thresholds; (5) mass personnel actions of 100+ career employees or 20+ Senior Executive Service.
SEC. 102. CONTINUITY & ACTING SERVICE RULES.
Clarifies acting official designations; bars circumvention of Senate advice and consent via serial acting designations; maintains mission‑essential operations lists filed with Office of Management and Budget/Office of Personnel Management; prohibits structural reorganizations absent statutory authority.
SEC. 103. ESSENTIAL SERVICES PROTECTION.
Requires continuity plans for public safety, veterans’ care, Social Security/Medicare operations, critical infrastructure, and national security support; Government Accountability Office/inspector general readiness spot‑checks.
TITLE II - IMPLEMENTATION SAFEGUARDS DURING IMPEACHMENT
SEC. 201. “FUNDS” DEFINED BROADLY.
For this title, “funds” include appropriated dollars, supplies, staff time, IT services, and use of Federal facilities.
Explanation:
Stops end‑runs where implementation proceeds using non‑cash resources.
SEC. 202. CERTIFICATIONS, Government Accountability Office/inspector general LANES, & EXPEDITED COURT REVIEW.
Before implementing any major action during an impeachment period, the head of the agency must certify legal authority, necessity, and cost impacts; transmit to Government Accountability Office, relevant inspector general, Office of Management and Budget, and Congress; actions become challengeable on an expedited schedule in the D.C. Circuit; courts may grant injunctive relief.
SEC. 203. SUSPENSION OF MAJOR RULE IMPLEMENTATION ABSENT CERTIFICATION.
Major rules published during an impeachment period are stayed unless certifications under §202 are complete; life‑safety and court‑ordered compliance exempted.
TITLE III - EMERGENCY POWERS APPROVAL‑TO‑OBLIGATE
SEC. 301. APPROVAL REQUIREMENT.
No funds may be obligated or expended to implement a
new
emergency declaration first issued during an impeachment period unless a joint resolution of approval is enacted within
7 legislative days
after notice to Congress.
SEC. 302. CARVE‑OUTS.
Immediate force‑protection, life‑saving actions, disaster response already underway, and court‑ordered compliance may proceed for up to 7 days contingent on written certifications; lapse absent approval.
SEC. 303. FAST‑TRACK PROCEDURES.
Any Member may introduce an approval resolution; privileged status; limited debate; no dilatory motions; guaranteed up‑or‑down floor vote in each chamber within 7 legislative days.
SEC. 304. PILOT & SUNSET.
Pilot applies government‑wide for
2 years
after enactment; report due at 18 months on effectiveness and recommended permanent standards.
TITLE IV - COMMUNICATIONS & PUBLICATION INTEGRITY
SEC. 401. FEDERAL REGISTER & Government Publishing Office TIMELINES.
Office of the Federal Register and Government Publishing Office shall publish executive actions promptly; improper delays are reportable to Government Accountability Office; agencies must provide machine‑readable texts, authority citations, and cost statements.
SEC. 402. RECORDS PRESERVATION & DISCLOSURE.
Mandates preservation of Presidential and agency records (including electronic messages and encrypted channels) related to any major action; establishes a litigation hold during the impeachment period; accelerates Freedom of Information Act processing for covered materials.
SEC. 403. Office of Legal Counsel/Office of Management and Budget/Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs TRANSPARENCY INDEX.
Requires an indexed, non‑privileged summary log of controlling Office of Legal Counsel opinions and Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs determinations relied upon for major actions, with dates, authorities, and scope; classified annex permitted.
TITLE V - OVERSIGHT, ENFORCEMENT & WHISTLEBLOWERS
SEC. 501. Government Accountability Office & inspector general OVERSIGHT.
Government Accountability Office conducts quarterly audits of certifications, emergency approvals, and publication compliance; inspectors general receive dedicated hotlines and may issue immediate compliance directives.
SEC. 502. BENEFICIARY & CONGRESSIONAL ENFORCEMENT.
Authorizes suits by affected parties and committees to enjoin violations of Titles II–IV; exclusive venue in D.C. District Court; 14‑day briefing; 7‑day decision target; direct appeal to D.C. Circuit.
SEC. 503. WHISTLEBLOWER & inspector general SHIELDS.
Strengthens OSC timelines; expands anti‑retaliation protections for disclosures related to covered actions; bars removal of inspectors general without cause during an impeachment period.
SEC. 504. DAILY CIVIL PENALTIES FOR SUBPOENA NON‑COMPLIANCE.
If any person fails to comply with a subpoena duly issued by the House of Representatives or any of its committees or subcommittees regarding matters within the scope of this Act, a daily civil penalty shall accrue beginning on the day after the return date until substantial compliance or lawful excuse is established by court order.
Amount:
up to $25,000 per day for senior executive officials and corporate entities; up to $5,000 per day for other witnesses, as set by the issuing committee and subject to judicial review for reasonableness.
Due process:
written notice; opportunity to cure; expedited application to D.C. District Court for enforcement and determination of lawful excuse.
Collection:
penalties are debts owed to the United States; Treasury shall collect; courts may order escrow, asset freeze limited to penalty amount, and attorneys’ fees for prevailing House parties.
Explanation:
Creates real‑time consequences for stonewalling; penalties stop the longer it drags on.
SEC. 505. HOUSE CIVIL LITIGATION AUTHORIZATION.
The House of Representatives is authorized to bring civil actions in the U.S. District Court for the District of Columbia to enforce subpoenas, deposition orders, document preservation, and certification requirements under Titles II–IV of this Act; to seek declaratory and injunctive relief; and to obtain expedited consideration.
Expedited track:
7‑day response; 7‑day reply; hearing within 10 days; decision targeted within 7 days thereafter; direct appeal to the D.C. Circuit.
Service of process:
deemed effective upon delivery to agency counsel or the Office of Legal Counsel for executive‑branch defendants.
Remedies:
contempt, coercive per‑diem sanctions, and reasonable attorneys’ fees for prevailing House parties.
Explanation:
Gives the House a clear, fast lane to court to enforce the rules in this bill.
TITLE VI - IMPLEMENTATION, BUDGET, SEVERABILITY
SEC. 601. EFFECTIVE DATE.
Effective on enactment; Titles II–IV apply prospectively to actions taken during an impeachment period beginning after enactment.
SEC. 602. AUTHORIZATION OF APPROPRIATIONS.
Authorizes such sums as necessary (FY27–FY29) for Government Accountability Office/inspector general surge, secure archiving, and dashboard publication; offsets from FSGG administrative savings and anti‑waste recoveries.
SEC. 603. SEVERABILITY; SUNSET REVIEW.
Severability applies to all titles; emergency approval pilot sunsets per §304 absent reauthorization; Government Accountability Office submits a comprehensive review at 24 months with recommendations.
Frequently Asked Questions
Does this block disaster response?
No. Life‑safety, active disaster response, and court‑ordered compliance are carved out with short, renewable certifications.
Is this a power grab by Congress?
It’s a time‑limited balance: Congress confirms new emergency spending during impeachment while day‑to‑day government continues.
Does this freeze all regulations?
No. It pauses
major
actions without basic certifications and transparency; routine and safety‑critical actions continue.
What stops end‑runs through “acting” officials?
Serial acting appointments and structural reorganizations are restricted; Government Accountability Office and inspectors general can flag violations for rapid court review.
Bill Text
A BILL

To safeguard continuity of government and public accountability during impeachment; to condition new emergency spending on rapid congressional approval; to require certifications and oversight for major actions; to strengthen records and publication integrity; and for other purposes.

SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE.
This Act may be cited as the “Impeachment Continuity and Accountability Act”.

SECTION 2. FINDINGS AND PURPOSES.
(a) Findings.-Congress finds that impeachment periods present heightened risks of disorder, record loss, and hasty actions; that continuity must be preserved; and that transparent, time-limited approval and certification mechanisms protect the public interest.
(b) Purposes.-To ensure continuity of government; prevent abuses of emergency authorities; protect records and publication integrity; and provide enforceable oversight with expedited judicial review.

TITLE I-CONTINUITY OF GOVERNMENT & OPERATIONS
Sec. 101. Definitions; “impeachment period”.
Sec. 102. Acting service rules; prohibited circumvention.
Sec. 103. Essential services protection.

TITLE II-IMPLEMENTATION SAFEGUARDS
Sec. 201. “Funds” include money, supplies, staff time, IT, and facilities.
Sec. 202. Certifications; Government Accountability Office/inspector general review; expedited judicial review.
Sec. 203. Stay of major rules absent certification; life-safety exceptions.

TITLE III-EMERGENCY POWERS APPROVAL-TO-OBLIGATE
Sec. 301. No obligation of funds for new emergency declarations absent a joint resolution within 7 legislative days.
Sec. 302. Life-safety and court-order carve-outs with 7-day renewable certifications.
Sec. 303. Fast-track procedures; privileged consideration; guaranteed floor votes.
Sec. 304. 2-year pilot; Government Accountability Office report; sunset unless reauthorized.

TITLE IV-COMMUNICATIONS & PUBLICATION INTEGRITY
Sec. 401. Federal Register and Government Publishing Office publication timelines; authority and cost statements.
Sec. 402. Records preservation; litigation holds; accelerated Freedom of Information Act processing.
Sec. 403. Transparency index for controlling Office of Legal Counsel/Office of Information and Regulatory Affairs determinations.

TITLE V-OVERSIGHT, ENFORCEMENT & WHISTLEBLOWERS
Sec. 501. Government Accountability Office audits; inspector general hotlines; compliance directives.
Sec. 502. Beneficiary and congressional enforcement; expedited venue and appeal.
Sec. 503. Whistleblower protections; bar on inspector general removals without cause during impeachment periods.
Sec. 504. Daily civil penalties for subpoena non-compliance.
Sec. 505. House civil litigation authorization.

TITLE VI-IMPLEMENTATION, BUDGET, SEVERABILITY
Sec. 601. Effective date; prospective application.
Sec. 602. Authorization of appropriations; offsets.
Sec. 603. Severability; 24-month review and recommendations.
