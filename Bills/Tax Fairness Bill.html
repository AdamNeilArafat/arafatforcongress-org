Bill 1 – Tax Fairness & Relief Act

Legislative Blueprint with Explanations

Section 1. Short Title

“This Act may be cited as the Tax Fairness & Relief Act of 2026.”

Section 2. Findings

  • The wealthiest 1% and large corporations exploit loopholes to avoid taxes, while working families shoulder unfair burdens.
    Explanation: Many billionaires and big firms pay lower effective tax rates than nurses or truck drivers. This bill corrects that imbalance so regular families aren’t subsidizing the ultra-wealthy.
  • No household earning under $400,000 shall face higher taxes under this Act.
    Explanation: Guarantees that working and middle-class families won’t pay more. All new revenue comes from corporations and high earners exploiting loopholes.
  • Revenues from this Act will fund job creation, community investment, and deficit reduction.
    Explanation: Instead of giveaways to the top, new revenue will strengthen local economies, build infrastructure, and reduce debt.

Section 3. Corporate Minimum Tax on Book Profits

  • Corporations with annual profits over $1 billion shall pay no less than a 15% tax on book income (profits reported to shareholders).
    Explanation: Some large firms report huge profits to Wall Street but pay $0 to the IRS. A 15% book minimum makes sure they contribute fairly, regardless of loopholes.
  • Ensures firms like Amazon and Netflix, which report profits but pay minimal federal tax, contribute fairly.
    Explanation: Levels the playing field so small businesses and workers aren’t carrying the load while mega-firms free ride.

Section 4. Stop Offshoring & Profit Shifting

  • Align U.S. taxation of foreign profits with OECD Pillar Two 15% global minimum.
    Explanation: Ends the “race to the bottom” by making multinationals pay a fair minimum tax abroad and at home. Keeps jobs and investment in the U.S.
  • Strengthen Global Intangible Low-Taxed Income (GILTI) rules to tax profits where they’re earned, ending the use of shell subsidiaries.
    Explanation: Stops companies from parking profits in tax havens like Bermuda or the Caymans. Closes a major corporate loophole.

Section 5. Targeted Relief for Families & Small Business

  • Expand the Child Tax Credit (refundable up to $3,600/child) and the Earned Income Tax Credit for low- and middle-income workers.
    Explanation: Proven tools to reduce poverty and boost household income. The 2021 CTC expansion cut child poverty nearly in half.
  • Simplify small-business expensing up to $5 million annually.
    Explanation: Makes it easier for small businesses to reinvest and grow without drowning in IRS paperwork. Helps Main Street, not Wall Street.

Section 6. Tax Fairness Credit

Replace high-end itemized deductions (e.g., mortgage interest on luxury homes, certain state/local write-offs) with a flat, capped credit available to all taxpayers.

Explanation: Ends tax perks tilted toward the wealthy. Everyone gets the same fair credit, middle-class filers benefit most.

Section 7. Transparency & Accountability

  • Require the IRS to publish an annual public dashboard showing effective tax rates of large corporations.
    Explanation: Sunlight on corporate tax bills keeps the system honest. Voters and investors can see who pays their share.
  • Modernize e-filing for universal adoption.
    Explanation: Cuts filing costs and reduces IRS backlogs. Easier for small businesses and households.

Section 8. Enforcement

  • Enhanced IRS enforcement authority against high-income evasion; no new audits for households under $400K.
    Explanation: The wealthy hide trillions through loopholes and offshore accounts. Strengthening enforcement collects what’s owed — but shields middle-class families from extra audits.
