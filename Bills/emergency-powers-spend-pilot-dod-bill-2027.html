DoD Emergency Powers Approval-to-Spend Pilot Bill (2027)

During impeachment-covered periods, DoD may not obligate funds for executive actions initiated under national emergency authorities without timely Congressional approval, except narrow life-safety and active-hostilities carve-outs.

Spend-Only with Approval Preserves War Readiness OMB Dashboard + GAO Fast Judicial Review 2-Year Sunset

TITLE I — PURPOSE, SCOPE, DEFINITIONS

Purpose. Pilot an “approval-to-spend” rule at DoD during covered periods for actions initiated under NEA/10 U.S.C./50 U.S.C. emergency authorities.

Covered period. Same definition used across the project (Minibus-1).

Actions covered. New obligations, reprogramming, or contract awards first initiated in a covered period and predicated on emergency authority.

TITLE II — APPROVAL-TO-SPEND MECHANIC

  • 30-day temporary window: DoD may obligate funds for urgent actions for up to 30 days post-initiation with an agency-head life-safety/operational certification to Congress and OMB.
  • Approval requirement: Beyond 30 days, continued obligation requires a joint Congressional approval or explicit appropriation/authorization line-item.
  • Exclusions: Force protection in active hostilities and statutory notifications already required under Title 10 remain unaffected but must carry an OMB Compliance ID for tracking.

Project history context: This mirrors your “NDAA window” pilot to test the model before scaling government-wide in the Round-Two clean-up bill.

War Powers & National Emergencies Resolution Templates

Pair this pilot with ready-to-file resolutions that force timely floor decisions on unauthorized hostilities or stale emergency declarations.

When to use

  • WPR (War Powers Resolution): Use when U.S. forces are introduced into hostilities/armed conflict without authorization, or when the 60–90 day clock is expiring.
  • NEA (National Emergencies Act): Use to terminate or disapprove an emergency that predicates the spending you’re gating in this pilot.

Template — WPR Withdrawal Resolution

Concurrent Resolution
Directing the President, pursuant to section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution (50 U.S.C. 1544(c)),
to remove United States Armed Forces from hostilities in or against <Country/Entity>.

Resolved by the House of Representatives (the Senate concurring),
SECTION 1. FINDINGS. Congress finds that United States Armed Forces have been introduced into hostilities,
or situations where imminent involvement in hostilities is clearly indicated by the circumstances, without specific statutory authorization.
SECTION 2. REMOVAL. Pursuant to section 5(c) of the War Powers Resolution, the President shall remove United States Armed Forces
from hostilities in or against <Country/Entity> not later than 30 days after the adoption of this resolution, except for forces
engaged solely in (A) protection of United States diplomatic personnel and facilities, or (B) the safe withdrawal of personnel.
SECTION 3. REPORTS. The President shall submit a report to Congress within 7 days and every 14 days thereafter describing actions taken to comply.

Template — NEA Termination/Disapproval

Joint Resolution
Relating to the termination of the national emergency declared with respect to <Subject>.

Resolved by the Senate and House of Representatives of the United States of America in Congress assembled,
SECTION 1. SHORT TITLE. This joint resolution may be cited as the "Termination of <Subject> Emergency Resolution".
SECTION 2. TERMINATION. Pursuant to section 202 of the National Emergencies Act (50 U.S.C. 1622),
the national emergency declared by the President on <Date> with respect to <Subject> is hereby terminated.
SECTION 3. EFFECT ON AUTHORITIES. Any authorities exercised solely by reason of that emergency shall cease upon enactment,
except as necessary to wind down obligations incurred lawfully prior to termination and to address an imminent threat to life for up to 7 days.

TITLE III — TRANSPARENCY & AUDIT

OMB: Publish a dedicated pilot dashboard (IDs, authority cited, obligation timeline). GAO: 60-day post-period audit. DoD IG: hotline/advisories.

TITLE IV — ENFORCEMENT & REMEDIES

Withhold apportionment; de-obligation/clawbacks; knowing violations referred under ADA; injunctive relief available (no damages).

TITLE V — JUDICIAL REVIEW

Three-judge district court; direct appeal; rapid briefing; no automatic stay.

TITLE VI — SUNSET & REPORT

Two-year pilot sunset. DoD + OMB report to Congress with metrics (response times, approvals, mission impacts) to inform the Continuity Integrity Expansion Bill.
