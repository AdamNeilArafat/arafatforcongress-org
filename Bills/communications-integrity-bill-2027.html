Communications & Authentication Integrity Bill (2027)

Require verifiable, standards-based publication and authentication of executive documents during covered periods. One OMB Compliance ID. OFR/GPO/GSA/CIO gatekeeping. Public dashboard. Narrow life-safety carve-out.

OMB Compliance ID Federal Register Integrity Seals & Certificates Hosting Controls Public Dashboard

TITLE I — PURPOSE, DEFINITIONS, COVERED PERIOD

Purpose. Make executive communications lawful, traceable, and auditable in impeachment-covered periods. Close workarounds by aligning publication, authentication, and hosting rules with a single compliance checkpoint.

Covered period. Begins on House adoption of articles of impeachment against a sitting President or principal officer and ends 90 days after final Senate disposition.

Key terms. “Executive document” (rules, orders, guidance, directives, adjudications, certifications, personnel actions); “Publish/host” (FR/CFR, official sites, official repositories); “Funds” (money and resources incl. staff time, IT, facilities, seals); “Compliance Identifier.”

TITLE II — OMB COMPLIANCE IDENTIFIER & SUBMISSION PACKET

OMB issues an ID only when the agency submits: (1) legal authorities relied upon; (2) plain-language impact statement; (3) agency-head certification; (4) internal controls checklist; (5) machine-readable metadata (issuer, authority, date/time, version hash, contact, docket link); (6) if urgent, life-safety certification.

Project history context: Mirrors Minibus-1 concept: a single, public checkpoint and serial number to coordinate OFR/GPO/GSA/CIO and drive GAO/IG audits.

TITLE III — PUBLICATION & AUTHENTICATION INTEGRITY

  • OFR: Refuse Federal Register/CFR publication without a valid OMB ID (or time-boxed life-safety tag).
  • GPO: Withhold printing/hosting for items lacking a valid ID.
  • GSA: Withhold official seals/stationery for non-compliant documents.
  • CIO Council: Issue routing rules to block deployment on .gov systems absent a valid ID.

Congressional Review Act Disapprovals (Rapid Rule Check)

Pair this Bill’s publication/authentication guardrails with the Congressional Review Act (CRA) to force up‑or‑down votes on covered rules with limited Senate debate.

  • Eligibility quick test: Is it a “rule” (incl. major) under 5 U.S.C. 804? Was it submitted to Congress & GAO? Are you within the 60 legislative day window (or the next‑Congress “lookback”)?
  • Fast‑track steps: Draft joint resolution → introduce → committee discharge/placement → privileged floor time (limited debate in Senate) → simple‑majority votes in each chamber → presentment.
  • Scope: Disapproval nullifies the rule and bars substantially the same rule absent later law.
  • Lookback: Late‑term rules near sine die can be reached early in the next Congress.

TITLE IV — USE OF FUNDS CONDITION & EXCEPTIONS

No ID, no spending. No funds or resources may be used to publish, host, or authenticate an executive document first initiated during a covered period without a valid OMB ID or properly filed life-safety certification (30-day limit, renewable with findings).

TITLE V — TRANSPARENCY, DASHBOARD, AND AUDITS

OMB Dashboard: Public, machine-readable list of IDs, agencies, titles, timestamps, status. GAO: 60-day audit report after each covered period. IGs: hotlines and advisories for patterns of non-compliance.

TITLE VI — ENFORCEMENT

Apportionment holds; de-obligation/clawbacks for improper obligations; knowing violations referred under the Antideficiency Act; limited private right of action for injunctive relief (no damages).

TITLE VII — JUDICIAL REVIEW

Three-judge district court with direct appeal; 60-day target resolution; no automatic stay; deference to life-safety facts only, not legality.

TITLE VIII — SUNSET & SEVERABILITY

Four-year sunset; severable; effective on enactment.
