At a Glance

  • Cost: Neutral overall with targeted administrative costs offset by savings and reduced waste.
  • Impact: Improves fairness, transparency, and service delivery for the public.
  • Who is covered: Agencies, regulated parties, and the public who rely on these services.
  • Safeguards: Life safety carve outs, sunsets and reviews, and court enforcement lanes.

Why it matters

This bill sets clear standards. It reduces gamesmanship. It gives the public a fair, timely, and enforceable process.

Will this slow down urgent work?

No. Urgent safety work and court ordered compliance continue with narrow and renewable certifications.

Is there new bureaucracy?

No. The approach is simple. It uses short certifications and public notice backed by independent checks.

Does this change taxes?

No. The focus is on standards, fairness, and better execution. Any costs are covered by savings and recovery of waste.

Oversight Quad Chart
Q1 · Resolution of Inquiry (14‑Day Clock)
Privileged request for specific documents from an agency or officer. If a committee does not report within 14 legislative days, it’s dischargeable.
Use when:
Specific docs are being delayed or withheld.
Steps:
Draft → refer to committee → clock starts → report or discharge → floor vote.
Output:
Record of compliance/non‑compliance + grounds for hearings or escalation.

Templates & Quick Snippets
Resolution of Inquiry - 14‑Day Clock
Paste‑ready, narrowly tailored. Identify the officer, the documents, and the date range.
Resolved, That the President, or the Secretary of <Agency>, is directed to furnish to the House of Representatives,
within 14 legislative days after adoption of this resolution, copies of all memoranda, emails, directives, and attachments
from <Date A> to <Date B> concerning <Specific Program/Action>, including authorizations, legal justifications,
and implementation guidance, excepting material properly classified, with a classified annex if necessary.
After adoption:
Refer to the committee of jurisdiction; the 14‑day legislative clock begins.
If not reported:
Privileged motion to discharge is in order.
Committee Report Directive - Follow‑Through
Short directive paragraph for your report text; pair with a Government Accountability Office evaluation line.
Directive. - Not later than 120 days after enactment, the Secretary shall publish a machine‑readable dashboard
reporting monthly on <Metric 1, Metric 2> by State and by program unit, and certify to the Committees on <X/Y>
that <Specific Compliance Condition> is met. Government Accountability Office shall review data quality and report to the Committees within 180 days.
More directive language
Surgical Rider - Narrow Spend Limit
Tie funding to a precise behavior; scope to a program element and fiscal year.
None of the funds appropriated or otherwise made available by this Act may be used to implement or enforce
<Specific Action> unless the head of the agency certifies to the Committees on Appropriations and <Authorizing>
that <Measurable Condition> is satisfied and publishes such certification in the Federal Register.
