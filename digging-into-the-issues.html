Top Issue #1

Universal healthcare

Healthcare is a fundamental right. The U.S. spends more per person than peer nations yet leaves millions uninsured or underinsured. It’s time to cover everyone with a simple, automatic system that puts patients first.

The U.S. healthcare system, by the numbers

  • Spending: about $13,000 per person in 2022, nearly double the OECD average.
  • Outcomes: lower life expectancy and higher infant mortality than peers.
  • Access: uninsured hit a low in 2023 then rose in 2024 with Medicaid unwinding. Millions still fall through the cracks.

Plan: cover everyone and align policy with health

  • Automatic universal enrollment. A national benefits floor for all. Supplemental options can remain for add-ons.
  • Reproductive freedom. Keep medical decisions between patients and doctors.
  • Prescription drugs. Expand Medicare negotiation, end patent gaming, and extend out-of-pocket protections more broadly.
  • Mental health parity. Enforce equal access, networks, and reimbursement.
  • Primary care first. Invest in clinics and workforce in underserved areas to catch problems early and locally.
  • Health in all policies. Use national health goals to steer food, water, air, and workplace standards in the same direction.

How we will get there

  • Step 1: Pass universal coverage with automatic enrollment and a defined national benefits floor.
  • Step 2: Expand drug negotiation authority and tighten patent rules to curb evergreening.
  • Step 3: Target grants to primary care and mental health clinics and training programs in shortage areas.
  • Step 4: Tie food, water, air, and workplace safety targets to national health metrics across agencies.

Top Issue #2

Lowering the cost of living

From housing to groceries and childcare, families need real relief that lasts. No new taxes on working people.

What is driving the strain

  • Housing: limited supply and slow, paper-heavy permitting keep rents and prices high.
  • Groceries: consolidation, long-term contracts, and hidden fees weaken competition and transparency.
  • Childcare: costs rival in-state tuition in many states, with too few available seats.
  • Utilities: energy bills remain elevated versus pre-2020, and upgrades can be hard to access.

Plan: more homes, fair markets, smaller monthly bills

  • Housing supply and permitting speed.
    • Federal incentives for modern, digital approvals with clear 30-, 60-, and 90-day service targets.
    • Flexible zoning near jobs and transit to add missing-middle housing.
    • Support modular and industrialized construction to cut time and cost.
    • Prioritize infrastructure grants for communities that publish and meet predictable permit timelines.
  • First-home affordability. Create a First Home Mortgage Facility to buy down rates toward ~3% for income-eligible first-time buyers, with transparent budgeting and paired down-payment help.
  • Groceries. Enforce antitrust, curb junk fees and most-favored-nation clauses, require standardized disclosure of retailer fees and supplier contracts, and strengthen regional competition and food hubs. Protect SNAP EBT from surcharge abuses.
  • Childcare. Expand and refund the Child and Dependent Care Tax Credit and invest in supply grants so providers can add seats without lowering quality.
  • Lower monthly bills. Bigger rebates for weatherization, heat pumps, and community solar. Streamline LIHEAP with data matching to auto-enroll eligible households.
  • Open doors to work. Reduce excessive licensing costs, recognize equivalent experience where safe, and expand apprenticeships and paid earn-while-you-learn pathways.

How we will get there

  • Step 1: Pass a Housing Supply and Permitting Modernization Act to unlock incentives for faster, digital permitting.
  • Step 2: Stand up the First Home Mortgage Facility with federal credit support and bonds.
  • Step 3: Direct DOJ and FTC to prioritize food-chain competition and enforce standardized disclosure of fees and contracts.
  • Step 4: Expand energy savings rebates and community solar with simplified eligibility and automatic enrollment where data allows.
  • Step 5: Launch a federal Open Doors to Work initiative with states and employers.
Top Issue #3

End corruption and special interests

Politics should serve people, not donors. I will not take corporate PAC money. End pay-to-play and put voters first.

Plan: clean elections, real transparency, strong enforcement

  • Ban corporate PAC and lobbyist contributions to campaigns and ban lobbyist bundling. Tighten coordination rules.
  • Public financing with voter vouchers. One voucher per voter. Campaigns rely on small donations plus vouchers.
  • Near real-time disclosure. Every campaign dollar reported within days in machine-readable form with strong penalties.
  • Close dark-money loopholes. Beneficial ownership and pass-through disclosure for political spending.
  • Stronger FEC. Balanced quorum rules, faster enforcement timelines, and clear referral authority for willful violations.

How we will get there

  • Step 1: Introduce a Clean Campaigns Act that bans corporate PAC donations and lobbyist bundling and mandates near real-time reporting.
  • Step 2: Pilot a federal voter-voucher program modeled on Seattle with state opt-in.
  • Step 3: Tie disclosure rules to IRS and SEC filings and require beneficial-owner reporting for political spend.
  • Step 4: Modernize the FEC with clear coordination rules, swift penalties, and streamlined referrals to DOJ for willful misconduct.

Tax Fairness, CSR, and Good Jobs

Three bills. One goal: make the economy work for working people. Close loopholes, hold chronic violators accountable, and open real paths to good-paying work.

Tax Fairness & Relief Act — Bill 1: Tax Code Reform

Make the tax code work for working families, not corporate accountants. Everyone plays by the same rules.

  • Corporate minimum on book profits. If you report profits to shareholders, you pay taxes on them.
  • Stop offshoring games. Align foreign-profit treatment to end shell companies and profit shifting.
  • Targeted relief. Expand the Child Tax Credit and EITC; simplify small-business expensing and compliance.
  • Tax Fairness Credit. Replace select high-end itemized breaks with a simple, capped credit for all filers.
  • Real-time transparency. Modern e-filing and public dashboards so people can see what the biggest firms really pay.

CSR Accountability Act — Bill 2: Corporate Responsibility & Penalties

Make exploitation more expensive than fairness. Reward good actors. Penalize chronic harm.

  • Corporate Responsibility Surtax. Triggers include extreme CEO-to-worker pay gaps, wage theft, repeat OSHA violations, layoffs during record profits, and repeat environmental harm. Surtax scales with severity and history, and phases down as firms improve.
  • Disclosure. Standardized public reporting on pay ratios, injury rates, and community impacts.
  • Work, pay, and safety. Escalating penalties for wage theft and repeat OSHA violators; criminal referrals for egregious harm; credits for raising wages, retaining jobs, and investing in safety; uniform data across industries.
  • Private equity & communities. Curb debt-loading models that strip assets and jobs; tie favorable tax treatment to job stability, pension protection, and post-acquisition safety.

Good Jobs & Skills Act — Bill 3: Workforce Pathways

Open more paths to good-paying work. Break down barriers while keeping standards strong.

  • Right-size licensing. Reduce unnecessary costs; accept equivalent experience where safe.
  • Expand apprenticeships. Paid earn-while-you-learn programs that lead directly to careers.
  • Support community colleges and trades. Align training with real employer demand.
  • Training incentives. Encourage employers to upskill workers and build career ladders.

How we will get there

  • Step 1: Strengthen book-minimum and foreign-profit rules; launch transparency dashboards. (Bill 1)
  • Step 2: Enact the CSR surtax with standardized disclosures. (Bill 2)
  • Step 3: Raise penalties for wage theft and repeat OSHA violations; criminal referrals where warranted. (Bill 2)
  • Step 4: Tie private-equity tax treatment to jobs, pensions, and safety outcomes. (Bill 2)
  • Step 5: Expand apprenticeships, licensing reform, and training pipelines. (Bill 3)
