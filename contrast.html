Choose People Over Power

WA‑10 deserves better than politics as usual. I’m not running for a title or a career. I’m running to fix what’s broken and make Congress work for the people who live and work here.

Who They Work For

For years, Washington’s 10th District has been represented by politicians who say the right things but serve the wrong people. They had almost a decade to deal with rising costs, failing healthcare, and the housing crisis. They didn’t. While families struggled, they focused on donors, lobbyists, and political favors. Enough is enough.

Representative Marilyn Strickland’s Donor Base

Eighty‑five percent of her campaign money comes from outside WA‑10. That means priorities set by people who don’t live here and won’t feel the cost of her votes.

  • Took money from AIPAC and corporate PACs tied to Wall Street, Big Pharma, and defense contractors
  • Voted to restrict student speech instead of protecting it
  • Opposed fair‑share tax reforms to make large corporations pay what they owe
  • Ignored rising costs while siding with companies that drove up prices

Who I Will Work For

If elected, I will work for the people of WA‑10 — not corporate lobbyists or foreign‑influence groups. Every dollar in my campaign comes from people who live and work here.

  • No PAC or lobbyist money
  • Full transparency: every vote, meeting, and dollar made public
  • People‑first priorities: lower healthcare and housing costs, protect jobs, and help working families get ahead

When you don’t owe the powerful anything, you can finally fight for everyone else.

Why It Matters

WA‑10 is facing the highest costs in years for housing, healthcare, and groceries while wages stay flat. That didn’t happen by accident. It happened because leaders stopped planning for us and started protecting themselves. They had eight years to fix it. Instead, they worked for their pocketbooks and special interests. We can’t afford more excuses. We need results that put people first.

A Promise to the People

If elected, I will fight for working families, not the powerful. I will put people before profit, neighbors before lobbyists, and results before talk. Change won’t come from the same people who caused the problems. It will come from ordinary people who have had enough and are ready to take back their government.
