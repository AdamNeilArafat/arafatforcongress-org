Explanations

AIPAC is a national lobbying organization. In campaign finance discussions, “AIPAC network” is a shorthand some analysts use for PACs and bundled individual contributions aligned with AIPAC’s policy goals. All contributions are domestic and lawful.

These static snapshots show patterns: a high share of out-of-district money and a significant PAC component. That speaks to incentives and alignment, not guilt. It’s about who a member of Congress is financially accountable to.

When about 80% of funding comes from national PACs and far-away networks, it creates a strong pull toward those agendas. That risks sidelining WA-10’s priorities — housing, healthcare, and fair markets — in favor of national donor interests.

This page shows patterns and connections. It does not allege wrongdoing or illegal activity by any donor or recipient. The point is transparency and accountability.
