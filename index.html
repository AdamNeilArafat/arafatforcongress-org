Meet Adam Neil Arafat

Adam served this country for two decades, including time overseas where he saw firsthand how other nations structure healthcare, education, and public services. That perspective fuels his belief that America can and should deliver better for working families.

At home, Adam and his wife have kids in local public schools. They both participate in the PTA and volunteer at school and community events. He understands the everyday challenges families face and brings that lived experience to the work.

Today, Adam continues his public service as a project manager supporting the county’s essential infrastructure—work that keeps our communities safe and running.

His campaign is rooted in working-class values: fair taxation, affordable healthcare, housing families can afford, and a government that answers to people—not special interests.

Money in politics is one of the biggest problems we face. As long as campaigns are fueled by corporate PACs and big donors, policies will serve those funders—not the public. When lobbyists and special interests pay the bills, everyday people pay the price.

Adam takes no corporate PAC money because trust should be earned, not bought. He believes government must belong to the people, and that accountability starts with who funds the campaign.
