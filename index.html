Adam vs. the Incumbent

  • Fighting for fairness: Adam will close loopholes, restore progressive taxes, and make big corporations pay their share. In 2018, Seattle’s head tax was set at $275 per employee and projected to raise ≈$47–50 million/year for homelessness and affordable housing. Amazon paused construction and business groups mounted intense pressure; the council repealed the tax, leaving the shortfall to be covered by strained services and other local revenues borne by residents. Sources
  • Powered by people: Adam refuses corporate and PAC money. Across her congressional career to date (2019–2024), the incumbent’s campaign has taken ≈$2.04 million from PACs (≈$1.35M business, $415k ideological, $278k labor). In the 2023–24 cycle alone, PACs were 44% of her campaign funds ($905,175). Her top 2024 industry was Pro-Israel ($150,980), with AIPAC as a top contributor ($145,379), and Amazon among top contributors ($21,793, incl. $7,500 from Amazon PAC). Sources
