This Is What Urgency Looks Like

When something in DC is urgent, it becomes a must-pass. It gets attached to budget bills. It becomes a red-line in negotiation.

Urgency shows up in leverage, not press releases.

If they wanted these to pass, they would have. They don’t.

  • Healthcare is urgent.
  • Housing is urgent.
  • Worker protections are urgent.

A Higher Standard

Co-sponsoring a bill is the bare minimum. On key issues with broad public support, even that hasn’t happened.

I will seek out and co-sponsor the legislation working families support, and fight to move it.

If a bill has majority public support and never gets a vote, that’s a choice. It’s choosing donors over the people.

I’m running to raise the standard.

What Fighting Means

Fighting means using leverage.

  • Attaching priorities to must-pass bills.
  • Making them red-line conditions.
  • Escalating when they stall.
  • Forcing votes when leadership would rather delay.

That’s how urgency shows up in DC.
