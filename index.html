The Standard in Congress

If you claim to support working-family priorities, you put your name on them and you use leverage to move them. Anything else is a statement, not a result.

The minimum

Co-sponsor the bills you claim to support and show up when the vote matters.

The job

Force action when leadership would rather delay. That is what urgency looks like in DC.

Fighting means using leverage.

  • Attach priorities to must-pass legislation.
  • Make them non-negotiable conditions.
  • Escalate when they stall.
  • Force votes instead of accepting delay.

I will co-sponsor the working-family bills this district supports and fight to move them. If you want receipts, I will publish them.
