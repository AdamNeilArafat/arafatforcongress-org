Why It Matters

Our country is at a crossroads. Our children deserve a future, not a failing system run by career politicians who prefer the status quo. Adam believes change starts with leaders grounded in our communities—working-class Americans who aren’t beholden to foreign interests, corporate sponsors, or lobbyists. It’s time to stop waiting and start building a future led by integrity, optimism, and results.

Meet Adam Neil Arafat

Adam Neil Arafat is a retired Army aviation maintenance program manager with 20 years of service. He led teams that kept aircraft fleets and airfields mission-ready, serving overseas where he saw how other nations run their systems and care for their people. That perspective shapes his belief that America can and must do better for working families.

At home, Adam is a parent with kids in local public schools and an active PTA volunteer. He knows firsthand the challenges families face balancing school, work, and rising costs — and he brings those experiences with him to the fight for fair policies.

After retiring from the Army, Adam continued his commitment to public service as a project manager for King County Wastewater Treatment Division. There, he helps oversee the infrastructure that keeps our communities safe and running every day.

Adam’s campaign is rooted in working-class values: fair taxation, affordable healthcare, housing families can afford, and a government that answers to people, not special interests. He takes no corporate PAC money because he believes trust is earned — not bought.
