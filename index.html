Adam vs. the Incumbent

  • Fighting for fairness: Adam will close loopholes, restore progressive taxes, and make big corporations pay their share. In 2018, Seattle’s head tax was set at $275 per employee and projected to raise ≈$47–50 million/year for homelessness and affordable housing. After intense pressure from Amazon and business groups, it was repealed—shifting the shortfall back onto residents through strained services and other local revenues. Learn more
  • Powered by people: Adam refuses corporate and PAC money. The incumbent’s career fundraising includes ~$2.0M from PACs (business, ideological, and labor combined), with the 2023–24 cycle alone showing ~$905k in PAC contributions and Pro-Israel as a top industry; AIPAC and Amazon appear among top contributors in cycle summaries.
