Adam vs the Incumbent

  • Powered by people: Adam refuses all corporate and PAC money; the incumbent accepted over $191,000 from PACs and still solicits donations from corporate lobbyists.
  • Fighting for fairness: Adam will close tax loopholes, restore progressive taxes and ensure big corporations pay their share. The incumbent opposed Seattle’s "head tax" on large corporations designed to fund homelessness services.
  • Community first: Adam served our country abroad and works alongside local families, teachers and veterans. The incumbent spent years representing business interests as CEO of the Seattle Metropolitan Chamber of Commerce and sides with corporate donors over working people.
  • Transparent leadership: Adam will make every vote and expenditure public. The incumbent’s campaign relies on special‑interest contributions—including pro‑business PACs—and refuses to pledge full transparency.
  • No foreign lobbying money: Adam will reject donations from lobbyists and foreign‑policy groups that distort our democracy; the incumbent has accepted contributions from powerful lobbying organizations and votes with their agenda.
