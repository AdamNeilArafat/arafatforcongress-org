Adam vs. the incumbent

Issue Adam Incumbent
Corporate and PAC money $0 corporate PAC money. Supports public financing and small donors only. Relies heavily on PAC support. 2024 cycle summaries show about 44% from PACs, with Amazon among top contributors.
Cost of living Backs antitrust enforcement, farmer supports to keep groceries affordable, and a first-home federal mortgage option around 3% for eligible families. Status quo approach that has not kept up with housing, grocery, and energy costs.
Universal healthcare Wants universal coverage so every family is in. The United States is the only one of the 38 OECD countries without it. No comprehensive plan to reach universal coverage.
Special interests and lobbyists Supports a ban on corporate PAC and lobbyist donations and real-time disclosure. Democracy vouchers for voters. Accepts support from organized PACs and industry groups.
Corporate accountability Backs a Corporate Social Responsibility surtax for bad faith actions like extreme pay gaps, repeated safety violations, or offshoring while profits soar. Business as usual with limited consequences for repeat offenders.
Progressive revenue for housing Supports fair tools to fund housing and homelessness services so the burden is not on families. Business groups fought Seattle’s 2018 head tax and won repeal after pressure from Amazon. Planned revenue for housing was lost.

Sources: Seattle head tax background and repeal — Axios, Wired, overview. Federal minimum wage has been $7.25 since 2009 — U.S. Department of Labor. Universal coverage context — Commonwealth Fund, OECD. Public financing model — Seattle Democracy Vouchers.
